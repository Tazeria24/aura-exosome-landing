## Part 3: Sourcing & Logistics Regulatory Checklist

*Cosmetics are highly regulated cargo. Ensure you satisfy these requirements during your importing phase:*

### 1. HS Code Classification (Skincare Products)
Ensure your shipping documents use the correct Harmonized System (HS) code to avoid customs delays and calculate the correct import duty:
*   **Standard HS Code:** **`3304.99.90.00`** (Beauty or make-up preparations and preparations for the care of the skin, other than medicaments).
*   *Note: Duties range from 0% to 6.5% depending on your destination country's trade agreement with China.*

### 2. Mandatory Supplier Compliance Documents
Before the supplier ships the cargo, request these files and save them to your compliance folder:
1.  **MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheet):** Required by air/ocean carriers to certify the formulation is non-hazardous and safe for transport.
2.  **COA (Certificate of Analysis):** Details the exact batch composition, heavy metal trace tests, and purity test results.
3.  **GMPC / ISO 22716 Certificate:** Proves the factory manufactures cosmetics under strict international sanitization and quality control standards.

### 3. Recommended Shipping Method
*   **Trade Term: DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)**
*   **Why:** For initial cosmetic imports, standard air or ocean freight will require you to register as a cosmetics importer at your local customs port and file complex declarations. Shipping **DDP** via an experienced logistics forwarder means the forwarder handles importing licenses, handles customs clearances, pays the duties, and delivers the cargo straight to your warehouse door with zero hassle.
